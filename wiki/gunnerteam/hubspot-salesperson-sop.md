## 1. PURPOSE OF THIS GUIDE

This document is your operating guide for Gunner's new HubSpot sales workspace. It covers every tab in your workspace, explains how to move contacts through the sales process, and tells you exactly what action to take at each step.

This guide covers:

- The Summary tab and what each module means for your daily workflow
- How to manage Leads from first contact through to a Deal
- How to manage Deals from Pitch through to Close
- How to use your task list, including the automated engagement reminders
- How to use your schedule view

---

## 2. WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

### The Old Way

Previously, all new customer contacts were created directly as **Contacts** and a Deal was often created immediately alongside them. This resulted in the Deals pipeline filling up with people who were not yet real opportunities — making it hard to see where actual revenue was coming from and cluttering the pipeline with early-stage conversations.

### The New Way

We now use **Leads** as the front door to your sales process. Every new contact automatically becomes a Lead first. You work that Lead — qualifying, communicating, and building the relationship — until the person is genuinely ready to move forward. Only then do you create a Deal.

**The three stages of a customer relationship:**

| Stage           | What It Means                                                             | Where It Lives             |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| **Lead**        | A person who has inquired or been reached out to. Not yet an opportunity. | Leads tab                  |
| **Opportunity** | A person you are actively pursuing with a real proposal.                  | Deals tab                  |
| **Customer**    | A person whose project is contracted and ready to build.                  | Deals tab (Ready to Build) |

This keeps the Deals pipeline clean. If it's in the Deals pipeline, it's a real shot at revenue.

### How Contacts and Leads Connect

Every Contact at Gunner has a **Lifecycle Stage** that automatically updates as they move through the process. You do not manually change the lifecycle stage — it updates on its own based on your actions in Leads and Deals.

| Your Action | Contact Lifecycle Stage |
|-------------|------------------------|
| Contact is created | **Lead** |
| You create a Deal from the Lead | **Opportunity** |
| Deal reaches Ready to Build | **Customer** |
| Deal is Closed Lost | **Win-Back** |
| Lead is marked Not Ready | **Win-Back** |
| Lead is marked Spam/Vendor | **Spam/Vendor** |

## 3. THE SUMMARY TAB

The Summary tab is your daily home base. Open it at the start of every day. It gives you an at-a-glance view of everything that needs your attention — tasks, stalled deals, and upcoming meetings — so you can prioritize before you start working.

[SCREENSHOT: Full Summary tab view]

### Left Side — Modules

#### Tasks

Your to-do list lives here. Any task assigned to you — whether you created it manually or the system generated it automatically — will appear in this module.

**Use it to:** Review what you need to do today before opening anything else. Click **Create a task** to add a manual task for yourself.

> All tasks are also visible in the Tasks tab for a full detailed view.

---

#### Outreach Activities

Shows a real-time log of recent activity on your contacts, leads, and deals — calls logged, emails sent, notes added, and stage changes.

**Use it to:** Quickly spot any activity that happened since you last logged in. If it says "You're all caught up," your slate is clean.

> *Note: The Manage Sequences link in this module is not currently in use. Ignore it.*

---

#### Suggested Tasks

Automatically recommended follow-up actions based on your recent activity. HubSpot surfaces these when it detects that certain contacts or deals may need attention.

**Use it to:** Check if HubSpot is flagging anything you may have missed. These are suggestions — not assignments. Use your judgment on whether to act on them.

---

#### Follow Up on Meetings

Shows contacts or leads you recently had meetings with who may still need a follow-up action.

**Use it to:** Make sure no one slips through after a call or site visit. After every meeting, take a follow-up action before moving on — log a note, send an email, or create a next-step task.

---

#### Recently Stalled Deals

Deals that have had no activity in the past 14 days will surface here automatically.

**Use it to:** This is your momentum check. If a deal shows up here, something needs to happen — a call, an email, a note. A stalled deal is a deal at risk. Click **View deals** to open the full Deals tab.

---

### Right Side — Widget

The widget on the right side of the Summary tab has two tabs:

#### Schedule Tab (within the widget)

Displays your connected Google Calendar. Your scheduled meetings, calls, and appointments appear here so you can see your day at a glance without leaving HubSpot.

#### Insights Tab (within the widget)

Your personal sales performance dashboard. Key metrics displayed here include:

| Metric                        | What It Shows                                                                    |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Revenue Attainment**        | How much of your monthly or quarterly revenue target you have hit                |
| **Your report this month**    | Number of meetings completed and how much revenue in the pipeline you've created |
| **Your activities this week** | Calls made, calls connected, emails sent, and emails replied                     |
| **Amount of Leads**           | Number of active leads not started assigned to you                               |

**Use it to:** Check your numbers. Know where you stand before your manager asks.

#### Activity Feed Tab (within the widget)

A condensed feed of recent actions across your assigned contacts, leads, and deals.

## 4. THE LEADS TAB

The Leads tab is your primary working view for the front half of the sales process. It shows all leads assigned to you, organized into columns by stage. This is where you will spend most of your time until a lead is ready to become a Deal.

[SCREENSHOT: Leads tab Kanban view]

### Lead Stages

Leads move left to right through the following stages:

| Stage | What It Means | What to Do |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| **Need to Make Contact** | Lead has been assigned to you. No contact has been made yet. | Reach out. Call, text, or email as soon as possible. |
| **Qualifying Lead** | The person has reached out or responded. You are in early conversation. They are real — not spam — and are interested in information. | Gather basic information. Find out what they need and when. |
| **In Communication** | There is active back-and-forth. They are engaged and you are in a real conversation. | Continue the conversation. Use the Playbook to guide your questions and fill in their details. Work toward a Deal. |
| **Deal Created** | You have created a Deal for this lead. | The Lead is complete. Work continues in the Deals tab. |
| **Not Ready** | The lead is real but not ready to move forward right now. They may want to revisit in a future season. | Move them here. The system will mark their Contact as **Win-Back** for future follow-up. |
| **Spam/Vendor** | The lead is not a real roofing prospect — wrong number, vendor solicitation, or bot. | Move them here. The Contact will be marked **Spam/Vendor** and removed from the active pipeline. |

### Moving a Lead Through Stages

To move a lead to a new stage, open the lead card and update the **Lead Stage** field, or drag the card to the appropriate column in the Kanban view.

> **Rule of thumb:** If you are having real conversations with someone, they should be in **In Communication**. If you are not sure whether they are real, they should be in **Qualifying Lead** until you confirm. Do not leave leads sitting in **Need to Make Contact** — contact them or disqualify them.

### The Lead Card

Each lead appears as a card in the Kanban board. From the card itself you can take quick actions without opening the full record:

- **Lead tags** — Colored labels on the card that identify where the lead came from or flag a lead status. Common tags include source tags (Referral, Estimator, Lowes) and status tags (Missed Call). Tags are assigned automatically — you do not set them manually. Use them for quick context before opening the record.
- **Quick action buttons** — Log a call, send an email, add a note, or schedule a task directly from the card
- **Preview sidebar** — A collapsed panel at the bottom of the card that expands to show recent activity at a glance
- **Customer name link** — The contact's name at the bottom of the card is a direct link to their full Contact page

[SCREENSHOT: Lead card with quick action buttons and preview sidebar]

### The Lead Sidebar

Clicking the **lead name** (the bold title at the top of the card) opens a sidebar panel. The sidebar is organized into cards:

| Card | What It Does |
|------|-------------|
| **Scheduling Tasks** | Quick buttons to log a call, send an email, add a note, or create a task without leaving the board |
| **Lead Address Lookup** | Update the contact's property address on the record |
| **Playbooks** | Access the qualification Playbook (see Working a Lead section below) |
| **Connected Deals** | View any deals associated with this lead |
| **Lead Stage Tracker** | Visual indicator of the lead's current stage and where it sits in the pipeline |
| **Connected Contacts** | View and navigate to the contact record tied to this lead |
| **Upcoming Activities** | Scheduled tasks and meetings for this lead |
| **Recent Communications** | Emails and calls logged on the record |
| **Recent Activities** | Full activity log — all actions taken on this lead |
| **Notes** | Free-form notes from your conversations |

[SCREENSHOT: Lead sidebar with cards visible]

### Working a Lead — The Playbook

When you are ready to move a lead toward a Deal, use the **Playbook** feature. The Playbook is a built-in HubSpot tool that opens as a popup guide directly on the Lead or Contact record.

**To access the Playbook:**

1. Open the Lead (or the Contact from within the Lead view)
2. Click the **Playbook** button on the record
3. A popup will open with:
   - A script guide for what to ask or say during the conversation
   - Fields to fill in the customer's information directly on the record
4. Complete the fields as you gather information
5. When the customer information is complete and they are ready to move forward, create the Deal

[SCREENSHOT: Playbook popup]

> Fill out as much of the Playbook as you can before creating the Deal. The more complete the information, the cleaner the Deal record will be.

### When to Create a Deal

Create a Deal when:

- You have had a real back-and-forth conversation with the lead
- The lead has expressed genuine interest in moving forward with a roofing project
- You have enough information to populate a Deal record (scope, property, contact details)

Do **not** create a Deal for:

- Someone who just filled out a form and has not yet responded to outreach
- Someone who is Not Ready or on the fence about timing
- Spam or vendor contacts

**To create a Deal from a Lead**, open the Lead record and use the **Create Deal** option. The Lead stage will automatically update to **Deal Created**, and the Contact lifecycle stage will update to **Opportunity**.

## 5. THE DEALS TAB

The Deals tab shows all of your active deals in a Kanban view, organized by stage. Clicking on a deal card opens the full Deal page where you can log activity, update information, and manage the deal to close.

[SCREENSHOT: Deals tab Kanban view]

### Deal Stages

| Stage                  | What It Means                                                                                          | What to Do                                                                                                    |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pitch Scheduled**    | A meeting or presentation has been scheduled with the customer.                                        | Confirm the appointment. Prepare your pitch. Log all contact attempts.                                        |
| **Pitch Complete**     | The pitch or site visit has been completed.                                                            | Follow up immediately. Send a proposal or next steps. Create a follow-up task.                                |
| **Expecting to Close** | The customer has indicated they intend to move forward. A contract has been sent or is being prepared. | Follow up on the contract. Keep the deal moving. Do not let it sit.                                           |
| **Contract Signed**    | The customer has signed the contract.                                                                  | Ensure all project details are logged.                                                                        |
| **Ready to Build**     | The project is confirmed and scheduled to begin.                                                       | The Contact lifecycle stage automatically moves to **Customer**. Hand off to operations. Transition complete. |
| **Closed Lost**        | The deal did not close.                                                                                | Mark the reason. The Contact lifecycle stage automatically moves to **Win-Back** for future outreach.         |

### Working a Deal

Each deal card contains the full context of the opportunity — contact information, deal value, associated activity, and stage history. Log every meaningful interaction here:

- Calls completed
- Emails sent
- Notes from conversations
- Next steps and follow-up tasks

> Keep your deals current. If a deal has had no activity for more than 14 days, it will surface in the **Recently Stalled Deals** module on your Summary tab.

### Moving a Deal Through Stages

Update the deal stage by opening the Deal page and changing the **Deal Stage** field, or by dragging the card to the correct column in the Kanban view.

Move a deal forward only when the stage criteria have been genuinely met. Do not move deals forward to make your pipeline look better — an accurate pipeline is how we forecast revenue and prioritize follow-up.

## 6. THE TASKS TAB

The Tasks tab shows your complete task list — both tasks you have created manually and tasks generated automatically by the system.

[SCREENSHOT: Tasks tab]

### Manual Tasks

You can create a task from anywhere in HubSpot:

- From the **Tasks module** on the Summary tab — click **Create a task**
- From any Contact, Lead, or Deal record — use the **Tasks** section in the activity panel

When creating a task, always:

- Set a **due date**
- Write a clear **task description** (what you need to do and why)
- Associate it with the relevant Contact, Lead, or Deal

### Automated Tasks — No Activity Reminder

The system monitors all of your assigned Contacts and Deals. If any Contact or Deal has had **no logged activity for one day** and **no tasks currently scheduled**, the system will automatically create a task assigned to you:

> **Task title: No Activity — Please Engage (Contact Name)**

This task is a signal. It means someone in your pipeline has gone quiet and needs attention. When you see this task:

1. Open the associated Contact or Deal
2. Review the last activity and context
3. Reach out — call, text, or email
4. Log the outreach as an activity on the record
5. Mark the task complete

> Do not let these tasks pile up. An unworked No Activity task means a contact is being neglected. Contacts that go cold are opportunities lost.

## 7. THE SCHEDULE TAB

The Schedule tab displays your connected Google Calendar in full view directly within HubSpot.

[SCREENSHOT: Schedule tab]

Use this tab to:

- See your full day and week at a glance without leaving HubSpot
- Review upcoming customer appointments and calls
- Confirm meeting times before making contact

> Your Google Calendar must be connected to HubSpot for this tab to populate. If your calendar is not showing, contact IT.
